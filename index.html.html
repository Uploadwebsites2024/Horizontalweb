Region & ways of working.

Hybrid by default, with regional variations. Pick your region below for office days, hours, hot-desking and local security protocols.

History

HZTL in the UAE — since 2018.

Dubai opened in 2018 with George Smith at the helm. Sitecore came first, with Salesforce following shortly after.

The growth has been wild — already on our third office to accommodate the expanding team and client roster.

/ Ways of working

How we work in Dubai.

Item How it works
Hybrid Office days 3 days per week in the office. Tuesdays and Thursdays are the set days — the third day is your choice. Remote the other two days.
Hours Working hours 9:00 AM – 5:30 PM. One hour for lunch.
Office Hot desking & meeting rooms We hot desk — pick any seat each day, move around, sit with people you haven't met. To book a meeting room, just add the room to your Outlook invite — it'll tell you whether the room is free or already taken.
Parking Office parking — rota Limited paid spaces in the building, allocated on a monthly rota. The new rota is sent out at the end of each month. If you're not on the rota, there's paid parking next to the building — arrive early as it fills up quickly.
Client sites Working at a client site Speak to your project manager or client point of contact about on-site days. Patterns vary by engagement.
Security Office door — closed at all times Use your access key, even during working hours. The door stays closed at all times.
After hours Out-of-hours access Inform Rima, Rowena and your manager in advance if you're entering outside regular hours.
End of day Last out, lights out Last person to leave switches off the lights and ensures the office door is securely closed.

/ Time off

Booking your PTO.

Same flow regardless of region. Get both sign-offs before logging in the HR system.

All regions

The 4-step PTO process.

Approval first, then system entry. This protects your project, your team and your records.

  1. Ask your Project Manager. Get sign-off on coverage and project impact before anything else.
  2. Ask your Line Manager. Once your PM has cleared it, get your line manager's sign-off too.
  3. Self-assign your time off in your timesheet. If you log time in Certinia, add the days to your timesheet under the PTO category before the period.
  4. Log it in the HR system. Submit the leave request in the HR portal so it's officially on record. Both managers will be auto-notified.

/ Your buddy

The onboarding buddy.

Every new joiner gets paired with an experienced team member from day one. Your buddy is your go-to for the small questions you don't want to send to HR.

Who they are

A dedicated team member

An Onboarding Buddy is a dedicated team member assigned to help new hires settle in. They provide guidance, answer questions and offer support to ensure a smooth transition into the team and company culture.

What they do

Advice, knowledge, introductions

Your buddy offers:

  • Advice and guidance on day-to-day work
  • Knowledge and info sharing — the unwritten rules
  • Introductions to people across the team
How it works

Assigned before day one

Your manager and HR will assign a buddy before you start. You'll be introduced on your first day. There's no formal schedule — buddies are available whenever you need them, especially in your first few weeks.

A buddy ≠ your manager

Different roles

Your manager handles your work, performance and career. Your buddy is the friendly face who tells you which coffee machine actually works and where the good lunch spots are.

/ Security & Compliance

ISO compliance.

Horizontal Digital operates under formal information security standards. Our IT and security posture is audited and certified — relevant for client procurement, RFPs and security questionnaires.

Standards

What we hold

ISO certifications cover our information security management system (ISMS), governing how we handle client data, internal systems, access controls and incident response.

For current scope, certificate copies and audit periods, see the Horizontal Certificates page on Confluence ↗

RFPs & client requests

Need our ISO certificate?

Certificate copies live on the Horizontal Certificates Confluence page ↗. If you need a specific letter or scope statement for a client RFP, raise a Fresh Desk ticket tagged compliance with the client name, the deal/SOW reference and the deadline.

Open Fresh Desk ↗

Day-to-day

What this means for you

Strong passwords, MFA on everything, lock your screen when you step away, never share credentials, only store client data in approved systems (SharePoint, Certinia, Jira).

If you spot anything suspicious — phishing, lost device, unauthorised access — raise a ticket immediately.

Audit windows

Surveillance & recertification

ISO certifications run on annual surveillance audits with full recertification on a 3-year cycle. If you're asked to support an audit (interview, evidence, walkthrough), it's a priority — please make time.

Need a tool you don't see?

All new tool requests go through a four-step approval to avoid duplicates and unmanaged licences.

Process · Tooling

Requesting a new tool

If you'd like access to a tool you don't see (e.g. ChatGPT, Figma, a new SaaS subscription), reach out to Meg and Lucy. The request runs through global ops review before purchase.

The flow

  1. Email Megan and Lucy with the tool name, the problem it solves and why existing tools don't cover it.
  2. Global ops review — checks for duplicate memberships across the org.
  3. Approval process — if the tool is deemed necessary, it goes for sign-off.
  4. Purchase & provision — once approved, the tool is purchased and access is granted.

IT What meeting notes / AI transcription tool can I use? +

Microsoft Teams is the only approved meeting notes & AI transcription tool at HZTL. It captures meeting notes, transcripts, action items and an AI-generated summary automatically — all stored in your Teams calendar and OneDrive.

If you don't have access to the AI features (Copilot in Teams), raise a Fresh Desk ticket and IT will sort you out.

⚠️ Do not use other tools like Otter, Fireflies, Read.ai, Tactiq or third-party meeting bots. They're not security-cleared and may store client-confidential conversations outside our perimeter. This is a hard rule.

Time How do timesheets work? +

Every Friday, log your week in Certinia against the correct project codes — billable, non-billable, PTO and internal time all get recorded.

Timesheets are how we track utilisation, bill clients accurately and report margins, so they need to be in by end of day Friday.

For the full walkthrough — codes, billable vs non-billable, PTO, the common pitfalls — see the Training page and open Time Management 101 under System training.

Missing a code? See the next FAQ below.

Time I don't see the code to log my time — what do I do? +

It depends what kind of code is missing:

  • Project / new business code missing — reach out to Rowena. She'll get the project set up in Certinia and the code assigned to you.
  • Project code exists but you're not assigned to it — reach out to your Project Manager. They'll assign you to the project so the code appears in your timesheet.
  • Company code missing (PTO, training, internal, admin) — self-assign the company code in Certinia on your timesheet.
People How do I nominate someone for Raise the Bar? +

Raise the Bar is the global Horizontal recognition programme — EMEA, India and US all take part. Winners are announced every 3 months.

To nominate someone:

  1. Go to the #raise-the-bar channel on Slack.
  2. Post the nomination — tag the person, explain what they did, and why it stood out (linking to specific moments or work helps).
  3. That's it. Nominations are reviewed and shouted out at the next RtB session.

You can nominate anyone in the company, including colleagues from other regions. There's no limit on how many people you can nominate, but quality of the write-up matters more than quantity.

Tip: nominate as the great work is happening — easier than trying to remember weeks later.

People How do I book travel for work? +

All work travel is booked centrally by Operations. There are four steps:

  1. Get email approval from your line manager. The email needs to clearly state the purpose of the travel — workshop, client meeting, event, etc.
  2. Complete the Travel Request Form and attach the approval email. Open the form ↗
  3. Operations reviews and books. They'll proceed as long as: minimum 1 week's notice (unless urgent and SLT-approved), line manager approval is attached, and the form is complete.
  4. You'll receive a confirmation email with flight itinerary, accommodation details, and any other relevant info (visa, tickets).

Lead time: at least 1 week's notice unless urgent and approved by SLT.

Urgent travel: communicate directly with your line manager and SLT.

Travel cost rejection: the cost is subject to final SLT approval. If there are issues with the cost and it's rejected, Operations will let you know.

Questions? Message Rowena Cardoz.
